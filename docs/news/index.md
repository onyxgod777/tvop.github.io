Every thought, feeling, word, and deed generates a specific vibrational cause. That cause accumulates energy, compresses into lived experience, and returns — amplified — as your destiny.

On this page, we track **current world events as observed causes** and project their **necessary effects** if those causes persist unchanged. This is not fortune-telling. It is **causal inference** — the same principle that lets a physicist predict a trajectory from position and velocity. See the cause, know the effect. Change the cause, and the effect dissolves.

### 🔥 US–Iran War — Eleventh Night, Iran Retaliates Against Gulf Neighbours, Trump Threatens Underground Nuclear Site

???+ danger "Observed Cause"

The conflict has now escalated beyond bilateral strikes: **Tehran launched retaliatory strikes against Bahrain, Kuwait, and Jordan** — neighbouring Gulf states hosting American military assets — widening the war's geographic footprint. President Trump escalated the rhetoric further by threatening to bomb a suspected Iranian underground nuclear site called **"Pickaxe Mountain"** "very heavily." Defence Secretary Pete Hegseth told the Senate Appropriations Committee the war has cost the US **$37.5 billion** so far — up from $29 billion in May — and requested an additional **$87 billion** in congressional funding, with $67 billion destined for Middle East operations. Hegseth's testimony was repeatedly interrupted by anti-war protesters and ended in a shouting match with a senator. Despite the escalation, Secretary of State Rubio stated the US remains willing to negotiate — while simultaneously signalling no let-up in military pressure.

??? warning "Projected Effect (if cause persists)"

    - **Nuclear escalation risk** — Trump's threat to strike "Pickaxe Mountain" introduces a new dimension; the effect of openly threatening an underground nuclear facility is to create a self-fulfilling dynamic where Iran accelerates its nuclear programme as a deterrent, precisely because the US has signalled it would strike such sites
    - **Congressional fracture intensifies** — Hegseth's $37.5B figure and $87B request landed in a hearing disrupted by protesters and ended in a shouting match; the effect is a growing legitimacy crisis where the executive branch continues a war that Congress and the public are increasingly questioning
    - **Financial cost compounds** — at $37.5B and climbing — with Democratic sources believing the true figure is much higher — the effect is a war whose economic burden will constrain every other US domestic priority for years, regardless of the military outcome

Rubio's stated willingness to negotiate while the bombs continue falling is not a genuine diplomatic offer — it is a contradiction. ### 🇺🇦 Zelenskyy Sacks Ukraine's Military Chief — Protests Force Wartime Leadership Crisis

???+ danger "Observed Cause"
    On July 22, 2026, Ukrainian President **Volodymyr Zelenskyy** fired **General Oleksandr Syrskyi**, the commander-in-chief of Ukraine's armed forces, following **days of widespread protests** triggered by the dismissal of a rival minister and deepening divisions within Ukraine's wartime leadership. Syrskyi's firing came after massive public demonstrations — the largest internal political challenge Zelenskyy has faced since Russia's full-scale invasion began in 2022. The protests were sparked by Zelenskyy's earlier decision to sack a popular minister, which critics saw as a consolidation of power at a time when unity was critical. Zelenskyy appointed **Mykhailo Drapatyi**, a younger general who earned his reputation 12 years ago with a daring tank manoeuvre, as Ukraine's new top commander. The shakeup comes as Ukraine continues to strike Russian infrastructure — hitting Wildberries warehouses in Krasnodar and Nevinnomyssk, an oil depot, and four ships of Russia's shadow fleet in the Black Sea — while Russian forces maintain pressure along the eastern front. The leadership crisis exposes a fundamental tension: how does a democracy maintain political accountability while prosecuting an existential war?

??? warning "Projected Effect (if cause persists)"
    - **Military cohesion tested** — changing commanders in the middle of a war carries inherent risk; the effect is a period of operational disruption as Drapatyi establishes his command authority and relationship with battlefield commanders, creating a window of vulnerability that Russian forces may exploit
    - **Political instability deepens** — the protests that forced Syrskyi's firing did not arise from military failure alone; they were fuelled by broader frustration with wartime governance; the effect is a precedent that mass protests can force leadership changes — which strengthens accountability in the short term but creates instability if it becomes a recurring pattern
    - **Western support questioned** — Ukraine's ability to maintain political cohesion is a key factor in continued Western military and financial support; the effect of visible leadership turmoil is anxiety among allies who may reconsider the sustainability of their commitments
    - **Russian propaganda opportunity** — the sacking of a commander during wartime provides Moscow with a narrative of Ukrainian disarray; the effect is both a psychological operations advantage and a potential real impact on Russian battlefield decision-making, which may sense an opportunity to press harder
    - **New strategy, new possibility** — Drapatyi's appointment could also bring fresh thinking; the effect of new leadership is unpredictability — it could reinvigorate Ukrainian operations or introduce strategic errors during the transition period

    *"If, however, you change the cause toward the good or positive, then the effect also changes."* — §110

    **The hinge:** The cause is not the protests themselves — those were an effect of accumulated grievances about wartime governance. The deeper cause is the structure of Ukraine's wartime political system, which concentrates immense power in the presidency while expecting battlefield results that depend on factors well beyond any leader's control. The hinge is whether Zelenskyy uses this crisis to strengthen Ukraine's institutions — broadening the circle of decision-makers, improving communication with civil society, and building the political resilience that a long war demands. Drapatyi's appointment offers a chance for a strategic reset, but only if the underlying causes of public frustration — transparency, accountability, and a credible plan for victory — are addressed. If the cause changes from top-down crisis management to inclusive wartime governance, the effect can be a more resilient Ukraine rather than a revolving door of commanders. If the pattern repeats, the effect will be a steady erosion of the very unity that has sustained Ukraine's defence.

### 🇸🇦 Trump Approves Saudi Nuclear Deal — Uranium Enrichment Without IAEA Safeguards

???+ danger "Observed Cause"
    On July 22, 2026, the Trump administration reached a **civilian nuclear cooperation agreement with Saudi Arabia** that would permit the kingdom to **enrich its own uranium** — a capability that has historically been the closest path to nuclear weapons production. The deal, which Trump will submit to Congress for approval, reportedly lacks standard **IAEA safeguards** that have been a requirement of previous US nuclear cooperation agreements with other nations. Critics warn the agreement includes no requirement for Saudi Arabia to normalize relations with Israel and no prohibition on developing enrichment capability beyond civilian needs. The deal comes at a moment of maximum Middle East instability: the US is in active war with Iran, Saudi Arabia faces simultaneous threats to its shipping lanes at both Hormuz (blockaded by Iran) and Bab el-Mandeb (threatened by Iran-aligned Houthis), and the kingdom has signalled it would seek nuclear weapons if Iran developed them first. Secretary of State Rubio defended the deal as necessary to wean Saudi Arabia off Chinese and Russian nuclear technology, arguing that a US-managed programme provides more oversight than the alternatives.

??? warning "Projected Effect (if cause persists)"
    - **Middle East nuclear arms race accelerates** — the US permitting Saudi uranium enrichment without full IAEA safeguards dismantles the non-proliferation architecture that has prevented nuclear weapons spread in the Middle East for decades; the effect is a cascade of similar demands from other regional powers (Turkey, UAE, Egypt) and a world where the "N in nuclear" — weapons — becomes a realistic national ambition for multiple states simultaneously
    - **Iran's nuclear calculus shifts** — if Iran's war with the US ends, the Saudi enrichment programme will justify Iran's own nuclear ambitions; the effect is that the post-war non-proliferation challenge becomes more difficult than the pre-war one — a perverse outcome of a war ostensibly fought to stop Iran's nuclear programme
    - **Congressional and allied resistance** — the deal's lack of safeguards will face significant opposition in Congress and from US allies, particularly Israel and European powers; the effect is a protracted political battle that delays implementation while the strategic damage to non-proliferation norms is already done
    - **China and Russia gain leverage** — the administration's rationale is that a US deal prevents Saudi Arabia from turning to China or Russia; but the effect of a weakened safeguards regime is that it undermines the very non-proliferation system that gives the US leverage to deny enrichment to other states
    - **Precedent for the global nuclear order** — the largest oil exporter on Earth acquiring enrichment capability creates a precedent that resource-rich states have a right to the full nuclear fuel cycle; the effect is a fundamental reordering of the Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty's bargain

    *"Through the accumulation of the energy and power which leads back to the thought-feeling-based cause, an ever greater compaction forms which the creator must experience."* — §118

    **The hinge:** The cause is a policy choice — the Trump administration chose to prioritise a strategic alliance with Saudi Arabia over the non-proliferation norms that have constrained nuclear spread for half a century. The administration frames this as realism: if the US doesn't supply the technology, China or Russia will. That framing itself is the hinge — is it true that Saudi enrichment is inevitable regardless of US policy, or is the US choice actively creating the very inevitability it claims to merely be responding to? The hinge is whether Congress insists on full IAEA safeguards before approving the deal. It is whether Saudi Arabia, when offered a genuine partnership that meets its energy needs without enrichment, would accept. It is whether the international community can devise a multilateral framework that provides civilian nuclear energy to states like Saudi Arabia without enabling weapons pathways. The current cause — bilateral deal-making that weakens the global non-proliferation regime — produces an effect that no single country can easily reverse. Change the cause — insist on safeguards, demand regional transparency — and the effect of proliferation can become an effect of responsible stewardship.

### 🇫🇷 France Bans Social Media for Children Under 15 — First EU Country to Act

???+ danger "Observed Cause"
    On July 22, 2026, the **French parliament approved a sweeping ban on social media access for children under 15** without parental consent, making France the first European Union member state to enact such a law. The legislation requires social media platforms — including TikTok, Instagram, Snapchat, and others — to verify users' ages and block access for minors under 15 who do not have explicit parental authorisation. Platforms that fail to comply face significant fines. The law was passed with broad cross-party support in the National Assembly and Senate, reflecting a growing consensus across European societies that social media's effects on adolescent mental health, attention, and development constitute a public health crisis requiring legislative intervention. France joins a growing list of countries — including Australia, Denmark, Greece, and Malaysia — that are either implementing or considering similar restrictions. The law adds momentum to a global push to protect minors online, with Portugal, Spain, and Italy also considering measures.

??? warning "Projected Effect (if cause persists)"
    - **Regulatory domino effect** — France, as the EU's second-largest economy, sets a powerful precedent; the effect is that other EU member states and the European Commission itself face increased pressure to harmonise age-restriction laws across the single market, potentially creating a de facto European standard for minors' access to social media
    - **Implementation challenges** — age verification at scale is technically complex and raises privacy concerns; the effect is a period of legal and technical friction as platforms develop and deploy verification systems that satisfy both the law and Europe's strict data protection framework (GDPR)
    - **Effect on teen mental health** — the law's stated cause is the documented harm of social media on adolescent mental health; if the law is effectively enforced, the effect will be measurable improvements in wellbeing metrics among French adolescents — providing the first large-scale evidence base for similar policies worldwide
    - **Platform business models disrupted** — under-15s represent a significant user base and future revenue stream for social media platforms; the effect of restricting access is a recalibration of platform design, content moderation, and advertising strategies in the French market — and potentially beyond
    - **Free speech and parental rights debate** — the law has critics who argue it infringes on both children's rights to information and parents' rights to make their own decisions; the effect is an ongoing legal and cultural debate that will test the balance between protection and liberty in the digital age

    *"The destiny which you determine yourselves through your thoughts and feelings, through your activity and your actions and deeds is based on consequences."* — §105

    **The hinge:** The cause is a legislative choice — French lawmakers decided that the harm caused by social media to children outweighs the freedom of both children and platforms to operate without restriction. This is a conscious intervention in the causal chain that has been running largely unchecked: platform design → attention extraction → adolescent harm. The hinge is whether other nations follow France's lead, creating a cumulative effect that forces global platforms to redesign their products for the lowest common regulatory denominator — or whether France's law remains an outlier, diluted by enforcement challenges and legal challenges. The deeper hinge is whether society chooses to treat social media addiction as a design problem — solvable by regulation — or as a human nature problem — solvable only by education and awareness. The French law assumes the former. If that assumption proves correct, the effect is a healthier digital ecosystem for children. If incorrect, the effect is a regulatory gesture that creates the appearance of action without addressing the underlying cause: platform business models that profit from attention regardless of age.

### 🌊 Tropical Storm Bertha Makes Landfall — Gulf Coast Spared Major Damage

???+ danger "Observed Cause"
    **Tropical Storm Bertha** made landfall on the US Gulf Coast on July 22, 2026, bringing heavy rain, storm surge, and strong winds to the Florida Panhandle and Alabama coastline. According to the National Hurricane Center and NPR reporting, **damage appears to have been limited** — Bertha did not intensify into a hurricane before landfall, and its impact was less severe than initial forecasts had warned. The storm arrives as **Hurricane Fausto** simultaneously formed in the eastern Pacific — a reminder that the 2026 Atlantic hurricane season is intensifying. Bertha's relatively mild impact stands in contrast to the pattern scientists have warned about: rising sea surface temperatures in the Gulf of Mexico, driven by accumulated atmospheric carbon, are creating conditions for storms to intensify more rapidly and carry more moisture. The Gulf Coast also hosts nearly half of US oil refining capacity and significant military assets deployed for the ongoing Iran war — making any major storm a compound disaster risk.

??? warning "Projected Effect (if cause persists)"
    - **Immediate relief, not resolution** — Bertha's limited damage is a fortunate outcome, but the effect of one mild storm in a warming Gulf is not proof of safety; it is a statistical reprieve that should not be mistaken for a trend
    - **Energy infrastructure stress tested** — while Bertha's impact on refineries was minimal, the effect of even a near-miss for Gulf energy infrastructure during an active foreign conflict is a reminder of how fragile the energy supply chain remains — one major storm during the Iran war could have caused simultaneous military and economic crises
    - **Climate signal persists** — Bertha was not caused by climate change, but its formation in a warming Gulf was consistent with the pattern; the effect is another data point in the accumulating record that extreme weather events are becoming more frequent and intense — even if this particular storm was mild
    - **Insurance and preparedness lessons** — every storm, mild or severe, tests coastal preparedness; the effect of a mild landfall is either a false sense of security or an opportunity to harden infrastructure and improve emergency response before the next, potentially stronger, storm

    *"Your self-created destiny is always and down to the finest and smallest nuance fair and corresponds to the effects of the creational laws."* — §119

    **The hinge:** Bertha's mild impact is a matter of meteorological chance — the storm did not intensify as feared — but the scale of damage it *could* have caused was determined by human choices made long before the storm formed. Building cities in storm-prone zones, underinvesting in coastal defences, failing to decarbonise the atmosphere, and placing critical energy infrastructure in hurricane corridors are all chosen causes. The hinge is whether communities treat Bertha's mild landfall as a warning to prepare for worse — hardening infrastructure, enforcing stronger building codes, and accelerating the transition away from the fossil fuels whose combustion is warming the Gulf waters — or as evidence that "it wasn't so bad." The same weather event, hitting the same coastline with the same preparation, could produce a very different outcome next time. The choices made between storms determine which outcome arrives.
