???+ danger "Observed Cause"

The US continued its **twelfth consecutive night of airstrikes** against Iranian targets, while Iran expanded its retaliatory strikes to include **Jordan** — a key US ally hosting American military assets — deepening the regionalisation of a conflict originally framed as bilateral. In a particularly devastating development, the **BBC visited an Iranian school where a US strike killed 120 children** on the first day of the war, reporting from the scene as grief-stricken families confronted the scale of civilian loss.     - **Regionalisation becomes irreversible** — Iran's decision to strike Jordan, after already striking Bahrain and Kuwait, creates a new front line; the effect is that Jordan — a country already hosting over a million Syrian refugees and dependent on US aid — becomes a permanent combatant, stretching both its military capacity and its internal political stability

The school's destroyed classrooms are not a prophecy — they are a verdict on choices already made. The next choice belongs to whoever makes it first.

### 🇸🇦 US–Saudi Nuclear Deal Signed — Uranium Enrichment Without Full IAEA Safeguards

???+ danger "Observed Cause"
    On July 22, 2026, the **Trump administration formally signed a civilian nuclear cooperation agreement with Saudi Arabia** — the so-called "123 Agreement" — that would permit the kingdom to **enrich its own uranium** without the standard full-scope **IAEA safeguards** that have been a cornerstone of US non-proliferation policy for decades. The deal was announced and will be submitted to Congress for approval, where it faces significant opposition from lawmakers who warn it dismantles the post-war non-proliferation architecture. The **New York Times** reported that the agreement has already prompted fears of a **global nuclear arms race**, with countries in Europe, Asia, and the Middle East now seeking nuclear capabilities to hedge against aggressive neighbours and what they perceive as a receding US commitment to non-proliferation norms. The administration's stated rationale is that a US-managed programme provides more oversight than the alternatives — China or Russia supplying the technology instead — and that Saudi Arabia, the world's largest oil exporter, needs nuclear energy to diversify its power grid and free up more oil for export. Critics counter that the absence of safeguards creates a direct pathway to weapons capability at the worst possible moment — with the US already at war with Iran over its nuclear programme.

??? warning "Projected Effect (if cause persists)"
    - **Non-proliferation architecture collapses** — the US, the architect of the global non-proliferation system, becoming the first major power to sign a civilian nuclear deal without full IAEA safeguards dismantles the norms that have restrained nuclear spread for half a century; the effect is a cascade of similar demands from Turkey, UAE, Egypt, and beyond
    - **Iran's nuclear calculus transformed** — the US went to war with Iran explicitly to prevent it from obtaining nuclear weapons; the effect of simultaneously enabling Saudi enrichment is to destroy the moral and strategic coherence of the war itself, giving Tehran a post-war justification for any nuclear ambition it may pursue
    - **Global hedging accelerated** — the NYT reports that the deal has already triggered hedging behaviour across multiple regions; the effect is a world where nuclear latency — the capacity to build weapons quickly — becomes the new normal for a growing number of states, creating a far more unstable system than the one the deal was meant to manage
    - **Congressional battle begins** — the deal requires congressional approval, and opposition is significant; the effect is a protracted political contest that delays implementation but cannot undo the strategic signal already sent to the world
    - **China and Russia gain a rhetorical victory** — the administration's framing that "if we don't do it, China or Russia will" is a self-fulfilling prophecy; the effect of abandoning US non-proliferation leadership is that China and Russia can now offer their own nuclear deals without the encumbrance of IAEA standards, making the world less safe while advancing their strategic interests

    *"Through the accumulation of the energy and power which leads back to the thought-feeling-based cause, an ever greater compaction forms which the creator must experience."* — §118

    **The hinge:** The cause is a conscious policy choice — the Trump administration decided that the strategic value of the Saudi alliance outweighs the non-proliferation norms that have constrained nuclear spread since the NPT was signed in 1968. The hinge is whether **Congress** insists on full IAEA safeguards as a condition of approval, restoring the standard that the administration abandoned. It is whether **Saudi Arabia**, offered a genuine partnership that meets its energy needs without enrichment capability, would accept limitations — or whether enrichment is indeed the kingdom's non-negotiable goal regardless of the partner. It is whether the **international community** can devise a multilateral framework for civilian nuclear energy in the Middle East that provides the benefits of diversification without enabling weapons pathways. The administration's framing — that the deal is inevitable because China or Russia will fill the void — is itself a choice disguised as a constraint. The hinge is whether that framing is accepted or challenged. If the cause changes — if Congress insists on safeguards, if allies demand a multilateral framework — the effect of proliferation can become an effect of responsible stewardship. If not, the effect is a world where the "N in nuclear" is no longer a taboo but a national ambition, and the war fought to prevent one nuclear programme has catalysed many.

### 🇨🇩 Ebola Stalks Crowded Displacement Camps in Congo — One Million at Risk

???+ danger "Observed Cause"
    The **New York Times** reported on July 23, 2026, that **Ebola is spreading through overcrowded displacement camps in eastern Congo**, where approximately **one million people** have been driven from their homes by ongoing violence. The epicentre of the outbreak is in North Kivu province, where families fleeing militia attacks have congregated in camps with **little to no clean water, inadequate sanitation, and minimal healthcare access**. According to the report, residents describe the impossible calculus of choosing between staying in violent conflict zones or fleeing to overcrowded camps where the virus spreads unchecked. Healthcare workers on the ground report that the outbreak is being exacerbated by a shortage of clean water — the single most critical defence against Ebola transmission — and by the difficulty of contact tracing in densely packed tent cities where families share sleeping quarters, cooking spaces, and water sources. The World Health Organization has raised alarms about the potential for the outbreak to spread beyond the region, especially as displaced populations move in search of safety. The outbreak occurs against a backdrop of chronic underfunding for global health preparedness and a Congolese government that has limited reach in territories controlled by armed groups.

??? warning "Projected Effect (if cause persists)"
    - **Exponential spread in camp conditions** — Ebola transmits through bodily fluids and requires rigorous hygiene to contain; the effect of one million people sharing scarce clean water in densely packed camps is a trajectory toward exponential case growth, as each infected person exposes dozens of others before symptoms appear
    - **Cross-border transmission risk** — eastern Congo borders Rwanda, Uganda, and Burundi, all with their own displaced populations and limited healthcare infrastructure; the effect of an uncontrolled outbreak in the camps is regional spread that overwhelms multiple fragile health systems simultaneously
    - **Trust deficit compounds response failures** — the region has a history of mistrust toward external health responders, fuelled by past experiences with inadequate or culturally insensitive interventions; the effect is that even when resources arrive, community resistance can render them ineffective — as seen in previous Ebola outbreaks in the same region
    - **Violence disrupts response** — armed groups operating in North Kivu have attacked health workers and treatment centres in previous outbreaks; the effect is that the security situation, which created the displacement in the first place, also prevents the public health response from reaching those who need it
    - **Global health architecture tested again** — the outbreak arrives as global attention and funding are consumed by the US-Iran war; the effect is a humanitarian crisis that unfolds largely out of the public eye, underfunded and under-resourced, until it reaches a scale that can no longer be ignored

    *"Your self-created destiny is always and down to the finest and smallest nuance fair and corresponds to the effects of the creational laws."* — §119

    **The hinge:** The cause of this crisis is not Ebola — the virus is a natural organism that has existed in reservoir hosts for millennia. The cause is a chain of human choices: the violence that displaced a million people, the failure to provide clean water to camps, the chronic underfunding of health systems, and the global distraction that leaves this outbreak under-resourced while attention is fixed on war in the Middle East. The hinge is whether the **international community** chooses to act now — deploying clean water infrastructure, funding contact tracing, and establishing treatment centres — before the outbreak reaches exponential trajectory. It is whether the **Congolese government and armed groups** choose to allow humanitarian access to affected areas. It is whether the global health architecture, built after the 2014 West Africa Ebola outbreak, has been maintained or has eroded. Each of these is a choice. The effect of not making them — of letting the current causes persist — is an outbreak measured not in cases but in thousands of preventable deaths. Change the cause — deploy water, ensure security for health workers, fund the response — and the effect becomes a controlled outbreak contained by the very tools that already exist. The cause is not the virus. The cause is the neglect.

### 🇺🇦🇷🇺 Ukraine Strikes Russian Wildberries Warehouses — War Brought Home to Ordinary Russians

???+ danger "Observed Cause"
    On July 22–23, 2026, **Ukraine launched a second wave of long-range drone strikes against Wildberries warehouses** inside Russia — hitting facilities in **Krasnodar and Nevinnomyssk** — marking a strategic shift in Ukraine's targeting policy. Wildberries is Russia's largest online retailer, frequently compared to Amazon, and serves millions of ordinary Russian households. The strikes hit commercial warehouses full of consumer goods — clothing, electronics, household items — rather than purely military targets. The **BBC and New York Times** both reported that this represents an escalation in Ukraine's air war, deliberately bringing the conflict to the everyday experience of Russians who had previously been insulated from the war's direct effects. The **Russian economy is already showing strain** from the war: Russians are reportedly turning to cash, putting further pressure on a slowing wartime economy, and recruitment of soldiers has grown increasingly violent and coercive. Meanwhile, Ukraine's newly appointed commander-in-chief, **Mykhailo Drapatyi** — who took over after General Syrskyi was fired following mass protests — faces the challenge of maintaining military effectiveness while navigating the aftermath of Ukraine's own political crisis. The strikes on civilian infrastructure inside Russia represent a deliberate choice by Kyiv to escalate the war's costs for the Russian population — a strategy that carries both tactical advantages and significant moral and strategic risks.

??? warning "Projected Effect (if cause persists)"
    - **War becomes visible to Russian civilians** — for two years, most Russians experienced the war through state media and economic indicators, not through destroyed neighbourhoods; the effect of striking commercial warehouses is to erode the social contract that has sustained public support for the war — as long as ordinary Russians felt the consequences only through inflation and casualty lists of distant soldiers, the war remained abstract; burning warehouses full of consumer goods make it concrete
    - **Economic strain compounds** — the Russian economy was already slowing under sanctions and war mobilisation; the effect of destroying major logistics infrastructure is to accelerate inflation, disrupt supply chains, and deepen the economic hardship that is beginning to generate visible social friction — including violent resistance to military recruitment
    - **Escalation spiral risk** — striking civilian-adjacent infrastructure inside Russia invites reciprocal escalation; the effect is that Russia may retaliate against Ukrainian infrastructure not previously targeted, expanding the war's destruction without changing its strategic trajectory
    - **Domestic Ukrainian debate intensifies** — Ukraine's own political crisis (the protests that forced Syrskyi's firing) was fuelled by questions about wartime governance and strategy; the effect of escalating strikes on Russian soil is to sharpen the debate between those who favour maximum pressure on Russia and those who worry about the moral and strategic costs of targeting civilian infrastructure
    - **Recruitment crisis deepens for both sides** — both Ukraine and Russia face growing difficulty recruiting soldiers; the effect of bringing the war home to Russian civilians is to potentially increase pressure on the Kremlin to end the conflict — but also to harden nationalist resolve and justify further mobilisation

    *"If, however, you change the cause toward the good or positive, then the effect also changes."* — §110

    **The hinge:** The cause is a targeting decision — Ukraine's leadership chose to escalate its strikes on Russian commercial infrastructure as a strategy to make the war's costs visible to ordinary Russians. This is a choice with a clear causal logic: if Russians feel the war in their daily lives, they may pressure the Kremlin to end it. But the same logic applies in reverse — striking commercial infrastructure invites retaliation against Ukrainian civilian infrastructure, and escalates the conflict into domains that both sides had previously treated as off-limits. The hinge is whether Ukraine's strategy succeeds in creating domestic pressure on Moscow to negotiate, or whether it hardens Russian resolve and justifies further escalation. It is whether the new commander Drapatyi brings a strategic framework that connects military operations to a clear political endgame, or whether the strikes become tactical escalation without strategic purpose. The deeper hinge is whether either side recognises that the war's resolution cannot come through destruction alone — that the cause of continued war is the continued choice to fight, and that to change the effect of endless war, the cause of endless fighting must be abandoned.

### 🔥 France Wildfires Force 12,000 to Evacuate — Europe's Climate Crisis Intensifies

???+ danger "Observed Cause"
    On July 22–23, 2026, **around 12,000 people were evacuated in France** as wildfires swept across multiple departments in the south of the country, driven by a combination of extreme heat, drought conditions, and strong winds. The fires, which broke out amid a prolonged heatwave, have destroyed thousands of hectares of forest and scrubland, threatening residential areas and forcing emergency services to deploy resources across a wide front. The evacuations come as **Canada simultaneously battles its own major wildfires** in northwestern Ontario, where residents have expressed outrage over inadequate official support and communication as flames spread. The parallel fire crises on two continents are consistent with the pattern scientists have long warned about: rising global temperatures, driven by accumulated atmospheric carbon, are extending fire seasons, intensifying drought conditions, and creating the conditions for more frequent and more severe fire events. The France fires arrive during a summer that has already seen record-breaking heat across Europe, with multiple countries experiencing temperatures well above seasonal norms. While individual wildfires cannot be directly attributed to climate change, the conditions that make them more likely — prolonged drought, higher temperatures, drier vegetation — are a direct consequence of decades of fossil fuel combustion.

??? warning "Projected Effect (if cause persists)"
    - **Evacuations become routine** — 12,000 people evacuated in a single fire event is a large number today; the effect of persistent climate trends is that such evacuations become a seasonal expectation rather than an emergency, placing enormous strain on emergency services, housing, and community cohesion
    - **Insurance systems stress-tested** — as fire seasons lengthen and intensify, the insurance industry faces growing exposure to climate-related losses; the effect is higher premiums, reduced coverage in high-risk areas, and an increasing role for government-backed insurance schemes that transfer the cost of climate inaction from private markets to taxpayers
    - **Health impacts accumulate** — wildfire smoke contains fine particulate matter that damages respiratory and cardiovascular health; the effect of more frequent fires is a cumulative public health burden that falls disproportionately on vulnerable populations — the elderly, children, and those with pre-existing conditions
    - **Parallel crises stretch resources** — France fighting fires while hosting major events and Canada simultaneously battling its own fires illustrates a broader effect: climate-driven disasters no longer happen in isolation; the effect is that emergency response systems designed for one crisis at a time are increasingly overwhelmed by simultaneous, geographically dispersed events
    - **Displacement becomes chronic** — for those who lose homes to fire, the effect is not temporary; rebuilding in fire-prone areas carries future risk, and migration away from high-risk zones represents a gradual redistribution of population that no government has yet planned for

    *"The destiny which you determine yourselves through your thoughts and feelings, through your activity and your actions and deeds is based on consequences."* — §105

    **The hinge:** The cause of the France fires is not the heat or the wind — those are natural phenomena. The underlying cause is the accumulation of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere from decades of human choices about energy, transportation, land use, and consumption. France, like every developed nation, built its prosperity on fossil fuels whose combustion is now returning as fire. The hinge is whether this summer's fires — and similar events worldwide — translate into **political action** that actually reduces emissions at the scale and speed required. France has invested heavily in nuclear power and has lower per-capita emissions than many comparable economies, yet it is still burning. The hinge is not whether France is "doing its part" — it is whether the cumulative effect of all nations' partial efforts adds up to enough, fast enough. The deeper hinge is whether human beings choose to treat fires, floods, and heatwaves as the **causal signals they are** — warnings that the current trajectory of energy use and consumption is producing the effect of a destabilised climate — or as isolated weather events to be endured until the next one arrives. The fires will eventually be extinguished. The cause that feeds them will not be, unless it is consciously changed.
