Every thought, feeling, word, and deed generates a specific vibrational cause. That cause accumulates energy, compresses into lived experience, and returns — amplified — as your destiny.

On this page, we track **current world events as observed causes** and project their **necessary effects** if those causes persist unchanged. This is not fortune-telling. It is **causal inference** — the same principle that lets a physicist predict a trajectory from position and velocity. See the cause, know the effect. Change the cause, and the effect dissolves.

### 🔥 US–Iran War — Ninth Night of Strikes, Ship Ablaze in Hormuz Strait

???+ danger "Observed Cause"
    The United States has conducted airstrikes against Iran for the **ninth consecutive night** as of July 20, 2026. CENTCOM announced the ninth wave of attacks targeted "Iranian military command centers, air defense and coastal surveillance sites, maritime capabilities, missile and drone launch sites, and communications networks." A third US soldier was killed in northern Iraq during a "controlled detonation" of an Iranian drone — bringing total US fatalities to 17 since the conflict began. Iran's IRGC claimed two oil tankers "exploded" and were halted in the southern Strait of Hormuz, warning that the corridor "is unsafe for the transport of chemical fertiliser or even a single drop of oil and gas." The United Kingdom Maritime Trade Operations (UKMTO) confirmed a vessel on fire northwest of Kumzar, Oman. US President Trump stated the strikes honour fallen American service members. Iranian Foreign Minister Araghchi said Tehran would end the war when it had "the upper hand in the field," framing negotiations as something that follows military achievement — not a path to avoid it.

??? warning "Projected Effect (if cause persists)"
    - **Hormuz chokepoint breached** — with oil tankers now ablaze in the Strait and the IRGC explicitly declaring the passage unsafe, the probability of a sustained disruption to 20% of global oil supply has moved from hypothetical to immediate; the effect is a global energy crisis that touches every household and industry on Earth
    - **Escalation without exit** — both sides have now framed themselves into positions where "victory" is the only acceptable outcome; the effect is a conflict that can only end when one side exhausts its capacity to fight, not when it chooses to stop
    - **Regionalisation widens** — IRGC claims an attack on Jordan, the US embassy warns Americans in Bahrain, and the conflict now spans Iraq, Jordan, the Gulf, and the Strait; the effect is a multi-front war that no single ceasefire can contain
    - **Human toll deepens** — 17 US service members dead, Iranian casualties uncounted, civilian populations in multiple countries now in the line of fire; the effect of sustained kinetic action is reciprocal grievance that compounds with every strike
    - **Global economic shock** — oil markets, shipping insurance, supply chains, and the energy-dependent economies of Europe and Asia all absorb the cost of a blocked Hormuz; the effect is a recessionary pressure wave that arrives before the physical damage is even assessed

    *"Cause and effect always belong together, they are one in themselves alone and are inseparable."* — §192

    **The hinge:** The cause is the bilateral commitment to military escalation as the sole means of resolving the dispute. Every airstrike, every IRGC warning, every "honour" justification is a chosen action — no cosmic law compels it. The ninth night did not have to follow the eighth. If either party chose a unilateral pause, a humanitarian corridor, or a neutral mediation channel (Switzerland, Qatar, the African Union), the projected trajectory of widening war would dissolve. Iranian FM Araghchi himself acknowledged that identifying "the precise moment to halt hostilities" is "the most critical decision in crisis management." That moment is a choice — and the effect follows the choice, not the calendar.

### 🇬🇧 Andy Burnham Becomes UK Prime Minister — Seventh PM in a Decade

???+ danger "Observed Cause"
    On July 20, 2026, **Andy Burnham** — the former Mayor of Greater Manchester, popularly known as the "King of the North" — became the United Kingdom's seventh prime minister in a decade. Burnham entered 10 Downing Street following a rapid succession triggered by the collapse of the previous Labour government under Sir Keir Starmer. His rise from regional mayor to national leader is unprecedented in modern British politics and reflects a deep public disillusionment with the Westminster establishment. Burnham built his reputation on pragmatic, visible governance — the Manchester Arena bombing response, transport reform, and housing policy — rather than national ideological battles. His premiership begins with a daunting in-tray: a cost-of-living crisis, an overstretched NHS, housing shortages, strained relations with Europe, and the ongoing US-Iran war threatening British interests in the Gulf. The speed of his ascent — from mayor to PM — speaks to the brittleness of Britain's political institutions, where governments now collapse faster than they can implement policy.

??? warning "Projected Effect (if cause persists)"
    - **Institutional fragility exposed** — seven PMs in ten years is not a succession of leaders; it is a symptom of a governing system that has lost its centring gravity; the effect is a loss of strategic continuity, where every government begins in crisis and ends before its plans bear fruit
    - **Regional vs. Westminster mandate** — Burnham's power base is the North of England, not the London establishment; the effect is a potential realignment of British politics away from the capital, but also a vulnerability to establishment resistance
    - **Immediate crisis management** — Burnham must navigate the Hormuz shipping crisis that directly affects British oil supplies and the UK's naval presence in the Gulf; the effect of inexperience at the national-security level is the risk of misjudgement under pressure
    - **Public expectation vs. structural reality** — Burnham's popularity rests on delivering visible local improvements; the effect of transferring that model to a national government with systemic fiscal constraints is either rapid adaptation or rapid disillusionment
    - **Populist pressure** — the revolving door at Downing Street feeds a narrative that "none of them can fix it"; the effect, if Burnham fails to deliver quickly, is further radicalisation of the electorate toward anti-system movements

    *"The destiny which you determine yourselves through your thoughts and feelings, through your activity and your actions and deeds is based on consequences."* — §105

    **The hinge:** The cause of Britain's revolving-door politics is not any single leader — it is the collective decision by the political class to prioritise internal party management over national governance. Burnham's election is itself an effect of that cause, not a break from it. The hinge is whether he chooses to govern differently — by building cross-party consensus on long-term issues (energy, housing, health), by insulating key decisions from electoral cycles, and by telling the public the truth about fiscal constraints rather than promising what cannot be delivered. A leader who changes the cause — breaking the cycle of short-termism — can change the effect. The same law that produces institutional decay, if the cause is reversed, produces institutional renewal.

### 🇮🇳 India's "Cockroach" Movement — 10,000 March on Parliament, Police Crackdown

???+ danger "Observed Cause"
    On July 20, 2026, more than **10,000 protesters** from India's youth-led **Cockroach movement** marched on India's parliament in New Delhi — the largest demonstration yet demanding the resignation of Education Minister Dharmendra Pradhan and a crackdown on corruption in the examination system. Police fired tear gas, deployed baton charges, and sealed metro station exits near the protest site. The movement's founder, Abhijeet Dipke, was detained. The protests, which began in May, have been fuelled by widespread anger over systemic corruption in India's educational testing system — including leaked exam papers, rigged results, and a bureaucratic establishment that has shielded the responsible officials for years. The movement's name — "Cockroach" — was adopted defiantly after a government minister used the term pejoratively to describe the activists. Thousands of young Indians, many of whom built their organising networks on encrypted messaging platforms, have turned the epithet into a symbol of resistance. The government offered a minor concession — inviting two activists to meet a cabinet minister — but the gesture came too late to defuse the momentum.

??? warning "Projected Effect (if cause persists)"
    - **Generational rupture** — when a government labels student protesters "cockroaches" and then assaults them with batons and tear gas, it does not suppress dissent; it converts it into durable opposition; the effect is a generation of young Indians who will not forget which side of the baton the state stood on
    - **Legitimacy crisis accelerates** — Modi's government, already under pressure from unemployment and inflation, now faces a visible, organised youth movement operating outside traditional party structures; the effect is a credibility gap that no policy concession can close if the underlying cause — corruption in the exam system — remains unaddressed
    - **Movement radicalisation risk** — peaceful civil disobedience met with police violence follows a well-documented trajectory: if the state escalates, parts of the movement either radicalise or fragment; the effect depends on whether the movement's leadership can maintain nonviolent discipline in the face of provocation
    - **Digital organising power** — the Cockroach movement built its infrastructure on encrypted messaging, bypassing traditional media; the effect is a new model of youth mobilisation that no government can easily control or co-opt
    - **International scrutiny** — images of Indian police beating student protesters, especially after the government's "Make in India" branding campaign, damage the country's soft power; the effect is a reputational cost that compounds over time

    *"Through the accumulation of the energy and power which leads back to the thought-feeling-based cause, an ever greater compaction forms which the creator must experience."* — §118

    **The hinge:** The observed cause is clear — systemic corruption in the examination system, the government's dismissive rhetoric, and the police response to peaceful protest. The hinge is whether the government changes that cause by opening a genuine accountability process — a transparent investigation into the exam corruption, the resignation or removal of the responsible minister, and a credible reform roadmap. If the cause changes from suppression to accountability, the projected effect of a generational rupture dissolves into one of institutional renewal. The movement itself also has a hinge: whether it maintains nonviolent discipline under provocation. Violence by protesters would change the cause and produce a very different set of effects — including a state crackdown with popular cover. The hinge is always a choice, on both sides.

### 🇻🇪 Venezuela Earthquake — Over 5,200 Dead, Anger Over Government Response

???+ danger "Observed Cause"
    Venezuela's catastrophic earthquake sequence — two powerful tremors that struck within 24 hours — has now claimed **over 5,208 lives**, according to official tallies as of July 20, 2026. The scale of the devastation has been compounded by a government response widely described as chaotic, inadequate, and politically self-serving. Survivors and journalists report that the interim government diverted rescue resources toward politically loyal areas while neglecting opposition-leaning communities. Amateur rescue teams — the self-organised "moles" of Venezuela — have carried out the most effective search-and-rescue operations, often without government coordination or support. There have been reports of looting, arrests of police officers involved in theft from collapsed buildings, and a propaganda campaign that minimises the death toll while exaggerating the government's relief efforts. Power outages, damaged roads, and overwhelmed hospitals have turned a natural disaster into a compounded humanitarian catastrophe. The Guardian's reporting captures the sentiment: *"God is punishing the politicians"* — a reflection of a population that sees the earthquake not as random geology but as the consequence of years of institutional rot and neglect.

??? warning "Projected Effect (if cause persists)"
    - **Secondary death toll** — the initial quake kills in seconds; the secondary toll from untreated injuries, contaminated water, expired medicine, and collapsed infrastructure kills over weeks; if the government's response remains incompetent, the effect is that the final death toll far exceeds the seismic toll
    - **Political collapse accelerates** — a government that cannot protect its own people in a crisis loses the last shred of legitimacy it may have held; the effect is accelerated political disintegration, whether through mass emigration, civil unrest, or de facto regional fragmentation
    - **Humanitarian vacuum filled by non-state actors** — when the state fails, gangs, foreign powers, and aid organisations compete for the role of provider; the effect is a transformation of Venezuela's power structure that no election can reverse
    - **Migration crisis reignites** — the earthquake has destroyed homes, schools, and hospitals in a country where millions had already fled; the effect is a new wave of Venezuelan displacement across Latin America, straining neighbouring countries already absorbing previous waves
    - **International response as geopolitical theatre** — aid from the US, China, and Russia will carry political conditions; the effect is that humanitarian relief becomes another front in the geopolitical contest for influence in Latin America

    *"Your self-created destiny is always and down to the finest and smallest nuance fair and corresponds to the effects of the creational laws."* — §119

    **The hinge:** The earthquake itself is not a human cause — it is a geological event. But the scale of the human devastation is not geological; it is the direct result of years of choices: underinvestment in infrastructure, corruption in construction standards, politicisation of emergency services, and the hollowing out of state capacity. The hinge is whether the government — or whatever authority emerges from this crisis — changes those deeper causes by prioritising transparent reconstruction, independent oversight, and competent administration over political loyalty. It is also a hinge for the international community: aid delivered without political strings attached would change the cause from geopolitical competition to genuine humanitarian relief. The effect would follow that choice.
