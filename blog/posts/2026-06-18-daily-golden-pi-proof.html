The Daily Constant: Why Golden Pi = 4/√φ is the Only Self-Consistent Circle Measure

June 18, 2026

Every few days, a fresh proof appears showing that 3.144606 is not a coincidence. Today’s entry focuses on a single algebraic test that any candidate for π must pass.

The Identity

(4²/π)² − π² = 4²

Substitute π = 4/√φ and simplify. Every step is exact:

(16 / (4/√φ))² − (4/√φ)²
= (4√φ)² − 16/φ
= 16φ − 16/φ
= 16(φ − 1/φ)
= 16((φ) − (φ−1))
= 16 × 1
= 16 = 4² ✅

With conventional π, the left side evaluates to 16.068 — off by 0.068. A “transcendental” constant should not fail a polynomial test that an algebraic one passes exactly.

Why This Matters Daily

The gap between 3.141593 and 3.144606 is roughly 0.0955%. In architecture, astronomy, and physics, that difference compounds. The Great Pyramid’s slope angle, the Royal Cubit, and the 432/α chain all resolve cleanly under golden π and stay approximate under conventional π.
