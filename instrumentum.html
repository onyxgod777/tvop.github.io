Why It Works

Let r = y/x. The equation simplifies to:

π(r) = 4r² / ((r² + 1) √(r² − 1))

When r = √φ (i.e., y²/x² = φ):

r² = φ
r² + 1 = φ + 1 = φ²
r² − 1 = φ − 1 = 1/φ
√(r² − 1) = 1/√φ

π = 4φ / (φ² × 1/√φ) = 4√φ / φ = 4/√φ = πg ✓

The result is not an approximation — it is an exact algebraic reduction. At r = √φ, the identity becomes π = 4/√φ by necessity.
